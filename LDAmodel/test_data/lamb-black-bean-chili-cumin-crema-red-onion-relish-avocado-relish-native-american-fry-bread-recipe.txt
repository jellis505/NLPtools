2 1/2 pounds lamb from shoulder, boned and cut into 1/2-inch cubes, Salt and freshly ground black pepper, 1 1/2 large Spanish onions, finely diced, 1 tablespoon chipotle puree, 1 tablespoon pasilla chili powder, 1 tablespoon ground cumin, 1 (12-ounce) bottle dark beer, Dash ground cinnamon, Cumin Crema, recipe follows, Avocado Relish, recipe follows, Red Onion Relish, recipe follows, Fry Bread, recipe follows, 1 tablespoon ground cumin, 1 tablespoon fresh lime juice, Salt and freshly ground pepper, 2 tablespoons canola oil, 1 tablespoon minced garlic, 1 serrano chile, finely diced with seeds, 1/4 cup freshly squeezed lime juice, Salt and pepper, 3 tablespoons finely chopped cilantro leaves, 1/2 cup red onion, minced, 1/2 cup chopped cilantro leaves, 2 limes, juiced, Salt and pepper, 1 tablespoon baking powder, 2 tablespoons dry milk powder, 2 teaspoons salt, plus more for seasoning after frying, 1 1/2 cups water
 	 Heat the oil in a medium saucepan over high heat. Add the lamb, in batches, season with salt and pepper, and cook until seared and browned on all sides. Remove the lamb to a plate with a slotted spoon. 		Add the onion to the pan and cook until soft. Add the garlic and cook for 1 minute. Add the tomatoes, chipotle, and spices. Return the lamb to the pan, add the stock and beer; cover and cook at a simmer for over medium heat, about 1 hour, or until the lamb is tender and the mixture has thickened. After 30 minutes, check seasoning and add honey. During the last 15 minutes, add the cinnamon and cooked beans. Season with salt and pepper, to taste. Sprinkle with chopped cilantro. Ladle into bowls and top with a large dollop of Cumin Crema, Avocado Relish, and Red Onion Relish. Serve with fry bread on the side.
